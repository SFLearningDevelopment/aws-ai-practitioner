Responsible AI & Ethics

Domain 6 · AIF-C01 · 11 Key Concepts
Domain 6
Concept Definition
Responsible AI Developing and deploying AI that is fair, transparent, explainable, privacy-preserving, and safe.
Bias (AI) Systematic errors in model outputs due to biased training data or flawed model design. Can cause unfair outcomes.
Fix: diverse data, SageMaker Clarify, regular audits
Fairness Model treats all groups equitably. No disproportionate harm to protected classes (gender, race, age, etc.).
Explainability / XAI Ability to understand and explain how a model makes decisions. Critical in regulated industries.
Tools: SHAP, LIME, SageMaker Clarify
Transparency Openness about how a model was built, what data was used, and how it makes decisions.
Privacy (AI) Protecting personal data used in training and inference.
Techniques: differential privacy, data anonymization, PII redaction
Robustness Model performs reliably across diverse inputs, including edge cases and adversarial inputs.
Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Including human oversight in AI decision-making, especially for high-stakes or uncertain outputs.
Model Card Documentation summarizing a model's purpose, performance, limitations, and ethical considerations.
AWS AI Service Cards AWS's transparency documents for managed AI services — describing intended use, limitations, and responsible AI practices.
Prompt Injection Attack where malicious instructions are embedded in user input to hijack LLM behavior. A key GenAI security risk.
